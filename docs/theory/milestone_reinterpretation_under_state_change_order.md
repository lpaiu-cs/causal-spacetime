# Milestone Reinterpretation Under State-Change Order

Milestone 18 reinterprets the earlier experiments as support tests for a
state-change causal trigger order program.

Milestone 19 adds the first toy implementation of the primitive layer itself:
a finite state-change trigger graph checked as a locally finite strict partial
order.

Milestone 20 adds observer-like chain selection diagnostics inside those
finite trigger graphs. It compares local-system chains, greedy order chains,
longest order chains, and random baselines as candidate observer protocols.
The comparison is based on coverage, two-sided bracketing, interval-profile
regularity, and ambiguity, not on metric calibration.

## Timelike Reconstruction

Causal interval count plus supplied density gives a proper-time-like
representation in controlled flat validation models. The metric scale comes
from density, not from order alone.

## Dimension Reconstruction

Relation fraction gives an order-statistical dimension estimate in controlled
Alexandrov intervals. This tests an order statistic, not a full derivation of
manifold structure.

## Observer Radar Reconstruction

An observer chain plus causal order gives operational radar decomposition.
Clock labels and the observer chain are supplied protocol structure. Radar
brackets can also be treated ordinally through tick positions.

Milestone 20 reinterprets the first step of this pipeline as a chain-selection
diagnostic: before radar brackets are used for distance protocols, a finite
network can be searched for useful observer-like chain candidates. The
best-scoring chain remains a hypothesis for a protocol, not a unique physical
observer.

## Oriented Radar And Atlas Tests

An observer protocol plus beacon reference can provide signed coordinate
representations in 1+1D validations. Multiple reconstructed charts test whether
overlap maps behave like Lorentz/Poincare-compatible effective atlas maps.

## Rindler Horizon Analogue

A restricted observer protocol defines a reconstruction-inaccessibility
boundary in a controlled flat-spacetime Rindler validation. This is not a black
hole model.

## Conformal Ambiguity

Causal order alone preserves conformal or light-cone structure in the
controlled models. Physical volume and clock scale require measure, density, or
calibration input.

## Measure And Coarse Graining

Density and calibration layers support metric-scale representation. Physical
measure can be supplied as weights or statistically encoded in sampling, but
absolute scale remains additional structure.

## Transport And Persistence

Same-position, velocity, and metric spatial evolution are protocol-dependent
predicates. Transport, anchors, persistence labels, or matching hypotheses must
be supplied or inferred before those predicates are defined.

## Ordinal Embedding

Distance order can sometimes admit a low-dimensional metric representation.
Ordinal embedding tests this as a finite diagnostic, not a representation
theorem.

These milestones are support experiments for the state-change causal order
program. They are not independent final theories and do not prove spacetime
emergence.
