# Reconstruction Program

The project's theory-facing objective is a mathematical reconstruction program:

```text
primitive information-accessibility
  -> causal order
  -> conformal/light-cone structure
  -> counting measure / density
  -> volume scale
  -> timelike duration
  -> dimension
  -> observer-dependent spatial decomposition
  -> orientation/reference protocols
  -> coordinate maps between observer protocols
  -> overlapping chart atlases
  -> horizon-limited reconstruction
```

The simulations reproduce and stress-test pieces of this program in known
settings. They are not intended to replace relativity, and they do not prove
spacetime emergence.

Milestone 4 adds dimension as an order-statistical observable through the
Myrheim-Meyer relation fraction. This is closer to the mathematical
reconstruction goal than simply validating a known metric formula in fixed
dimension, but it remains a controlled flat-interval test.

Milestone 5 adds observer-chain radar reconstruction. A supplied observer chain
with clock labels is combined with causal accessibility to reconstruct radar
time and radar distance. This tests the observer-dependent spatial
decomposition layer of the program while keeping the observer protocol explicit
as additional structure.

Milestone 6 adds orientation/reference protocol structure. A synchronized
beacon chain removes the single-observer reflection degeneracy and allows a
signed 1+1D coordinate to be reconstructed in a controlled validation setting.
Two such oriented inertial protocols can then be compared by fitting a Lorentz
map between their reconstructed coordinates.

Milestone 7 adds observer-atlas consistency. Multiple oriented protocols are
used to reconstruct overlapping charts from the same causal order, and their
transition maps are fit as affine Lorentz/Poincare maps. The experiment checks
transition residuals, invariant interval agreement, and loop consistency.

Milestone 8 adds a Rindler horizon analogue in flat spacetime. A supplied
accelerated observer chain and clock labels define a two-way radar protocol.
The experiment tests whether reconstruction-inaccessibility tracks finite-chain
coverage and the observer-dependent Rindler wedge.

The bold objective is to formulate coherent reconstruction maps and identify
their assumptions, limitations, and failure modes.
