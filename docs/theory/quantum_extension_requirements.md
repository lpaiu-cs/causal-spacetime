# Quantum Extension Requirements

The current work does not derive quantum mechanics. It studies causal
state-change order, observer protocols, order-level distance diagnostics, and
effective metric representations.

## Missing Quantum Ingredients

A quantum-compatible extension would need additional structure, including:

- complex amplitudes,
- superposition,
- interference,
- unitarity or a replacement evolution rule,
- Born rule,
- entanglement,
- measurement or update structure.

None of these are implemented in Milestone 18.

## Possible Future Direction

A future extension might study:

```text
causal state-change histories
  + amplitude layer
  + interference rule
  + Born-rule-like probability map
```

This would be a new layer, not something obtained from the present causal-order
experiments alone. Quantum compatibility likely requires a Hilbert-space,
amplitude, or equivalent probabilistic structure beyond the current
state-change trigger order.
