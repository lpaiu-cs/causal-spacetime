# Chain-selector robustness probe: the verdict does not depend on the selector

Date: 2026-07-17 KST. Status: **descriptive reconnaissance — zero verdict
flips across all variants; nothing frozen, no gate or pipeline constant
modified.** Probe: `experiments/positive_control/selector_robustness_probe.py`;
full per-run table with configuration in
`docs/selector_robustness_probe_results.json` (deterministic seeds; the
audit-trail convention of PR #8). Regression pins in
`tests/test_selector_robustness_probe.py`.

## Question

Paper B Section 11 lists "robustness of the verdict to the chain-selection
rule (number of chains, tick threshold, non-greedy extraction)" as an open
extension, and the P7 re-scope reconnaissance
(docs/p7_fss_rescope_recon.md) showed the greedy selector is a real degree
of freedom: its N = 200-400 structural blocks are selector properties, not
order properties. The complementary question inside the operating window:
**at N = 600, does the frozen PASS/BLOCK verdict move when the
chain-selection stage — and only that stage — is varied?**

## Design (fixed before any result was read)

- Downstream of chain selection, the frozen pipeline verbatim: bracketed
  targets (max 44, min 20), dissimilarity, fit (1500 steps, 5 restarts),
  gates held-out 0.10 / null gap 0.10 / truth 0.40, truth from 8000 pair
  comparisons.
- Variants (frozen protocol = greedy, 6 chains x >= 25 ticks):
  chain count {4, 6, 8} and tick threshold {20, 25, 30} around the frozen
  point, one axis at a time, plus a non-greedy **anchored** selector at
  (6, 25) with three selector seeds — each chain is the longest chain
  through a uniformly random anchor element, so chain placement is
  randomized and greedy whole-chain consumption is broken.
- Families, regenerated exactly from committed constructions:
  20 fresh uniform positives (descriptive seeds 200-219), the eight frozen
  P6a layered cells on confirmatory seeds 100-104 (40 orders), the
  bipartite crystal, and the 18 frozen P5 continuum samples regenerated by
  the trajectory-equivalent accelerated replay and provenance-checked
  against the frozen chain mean actions.
- Verdict criterion, fixed in advance: a variant *flips* a family if its
  majority verdict among numerically valid runs differs from the frozen
  selector's. Structural blocks are operability changes, not verdicts —
  the same separation the P7 probe used.

## Result: zero flips

| family | frozen (6x25 greedy) | worst variant | anchored (3 seeds) |
|---|---|---|---|
| uniform positives | 20/20 pass | 19/20 pass (4x25; anchored) | 19/20 pass each |
| P5 continuum samples | 18/18 pass | 17/18 pass (4x25) | **18/18 pass each** |
| P6a layered negatives | 3/40 pass (blocks) | 1-3/40 pass across all | 1-3/40 pass |
| crystal | structural block | structural block everywhere | structural block |

- The **verdict never flipped**: every variant that evaluates keeps
  uniform and P5-continuum majorities passing and layered majorities
  blocking; the crystal never yields a single chain under any selector.
  The layered pass rate (1-3/40 per variant) matches the frozen
  confirmatory rate (11/160).
- **Operability and verdict separate cleanly.** Demanding 8 chains or a
  30-tick threshold pushes positives into structural blocks (9-17/20
  uniform, 11-16/18 P5) — the greedy-consumption effect already measured
  by the P7 reconnaissance — but where the instrument operates, the
  verdict is unchanged (all surviving positives pass, e.g. 3/3 and 2/2
  at 6x30).
- Lowering the tick threshold to 20 leaves the greedy-selected chains
  (hence every number) unchanged at this scale: at N = 600 the six
  greedy chains are always longer than both thresholds.
- Gate metrics barely move across selectors: uniform held-out median
  0.036-0.050 (gate 0.10), null gap 0.166-0.221 (gate 0.10), truth
  0.110-0.218 (gate 0.40); layered held-out median 0.137-0.163 (above
  the gate) with null gap 0.013-0.073 (below it).

## Scope notes

- Descriptive, not confirmatory: seeds and the variant grid were fixed in
  the committed probe configuration before any result was read; no frozen
  artifact, gate, or expectation was modified; the probe answers a
  robustness question about the existing verdicts, it does not create new
  pass/block claims.
- The uniform positives use fresh descriptive seeds (200-219), disjoint
  from every frozen seed range; the layered and P5 inputs are the frozen
  constructions/samples themselves.
- Observer-congruence robustness (the scene-side analogue) remains open —
  recorded in Paper B Section 11.
- Cost: 2986 s single-core, of which the P5 replays are the bulk.
